<p><p>Membrane technology for liquid and gas separations encompasses various processes, each targeting specific separation needs. Reverse osmosis utilizes pressure to separate dissolved solids from liquids, while nanofiltration targets smaller molecules and divalent ions. Ultrafiltration focuses on separating macromolecules and suspended solids, and microfiltration removes larger particles and microorganisms. Additionally, electrochemical processes apply electric fields for selective separation, enhancing efficiency in applications like water treatment and gas purification. Each method plays a crucial role in addressing environmental and industrial challenges.</p></p>
