<p><p>Transdermal drug delivery involves administering medications through the skin for systemic effects, typically using patches that facilitate the absorption of drugs into the bloodstream. This method offers advantages like bypassing the digestive system and providing controlled release. </p><p>Transmucosal drug delivery, on the other hand, delivers drugs through mucous membranes, such as those in the mouth or nasal passages, allowing for rapid absorption into the bloodstream. This approach is ideal for drugs that require quick onset of action, enhancing patient compliance and convenience.</p></p>
