<p><p>The computer chip market includes two key types: Northbridge and Southbridge chips. Northbridge chips manage high-speed communication between the CPU, RAM, and graphics card, facilitating fast data transfer and processing. In contrast, Southbridge chips handle slower functions, such as I/O operations, USB connections, and storage interfaces. Together, these chips form the core of a computer's motherboard architecture, ensuring efficient performance and connectivity. The market for these components is driven by advancements in technology and increasing demand for high-performance computing.</p></p>
