<p><p>The hollow fiber ceramic membrane market comprises three key filtration types: ultrafiltration, microfiltration, and nanofiltration. Ultrafiltration targets larger particles and macromolecules, effectively removing bacteria and colloids from liquids. Microfiltration operates at a coarser level, capturing suspended solids and larger microorganisms. Nanofiltration, with its finer pores, separates smaller molecules while allowing divalent ions to pass, making it ideal for water softening and organic removal. Each type serves distinct applications in water treatment, food processing, and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
