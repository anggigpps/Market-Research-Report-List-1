<p><ul><li>Contacting</li><li>Electrodeless</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Conductivity analyzers are crucial in measuring the conductivity of liquids, with market types including contacting and electrodeless analyzers. Contacting analyzers utilize electrodes that come into direct contact with the liquid, providing precise measurements but requiring maintenance due to fouling. Electrodeless analyzers, on the other hand, use non-contact methods, often employing inductive techniques, thus minimizing maintenance needs and contamination risks. Other types may include portable or inline systems, catering to specific industrial or laboratory applications while ensuring reliability and accuracy in conductivity measurement.</p></p>
